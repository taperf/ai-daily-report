### Aaron Levie | Box CEO

Two substantive posts. First, Levie made a sharp point about why code is the ideal domain for AI agents: it's testable. You can quickly verify if an agent's output works. Most other work — trading, contract negotiation, sales pitches — only gets tested when it hits the real world. This creates a natural gap that evals will need to fill. He argued that "the enterprises that are able to eval their knowledge work the best also stand to gain the most from AI." Second, he endorsed a proposal for an AI standards body (distinct from a regulatory agency), warning that if AI progress slows to "the speed that the government classically operates at," America will "essentially guarantee progress begins to stall" and lose the AI race.

Box CEO Aaron Levie 发了两条有深度的推文。第一条他指出，代码是 AI agent 最理想的领域，因为它可被测试——你能快速验证 agent 的输出是否有效。而大多数其他工作（交易、合同谈判、销售推介）只有在真实世界中执行后才能验证。这创造了一个自然 gap，需要 eval 来填补。他认为"那些最能评估自己知识工作的企业，也将从 AI 中获得最大收益"。第二条他支持建立一个 AI 标准机构（区别于监管机构），警告说如果 AI 进展放缓到"政府经典运作速度"，美国将"实质上保证进展开始停滞"并输掉 AI 竞赛。

### Ryo Lu | Design @ Cursor

A long, deeply personal essay on what it means when AI starts doing the work you once loved. Lu wrote about the quiet pain of watching machines get good at the parts of creativity that felt closest to the heart — writing, coding, designing, reasoning. But the core insight is this: "AI can make the output faster... but it cannot want on your behalf. It cannot decide what is worth loving." The discipline, he argues, is not to work harder or care less — it's to keep returning to "the place where the love began" before it became useful, legible, or profitable. The post resonated strongly (809 likes), striking a nerve in a community anxious about meaning in the agent era.

Cursor 设计师 Ryo Lu 发了一篇长文，探讨当 AI 开始做你曾经热爱的工作时意味着什么。他写到，看着机器在写作、编码、设计、推理这些曾感觉最贴近内心的领域变得越来越好，有一种"安静的痛苦"。但核心洞察是："AI 可以让输出更快……但它不能替你渴望。它不能决定什么值得去爱。"他认为，真正的自律不是更努力工作或更少在意，而是不断回到"爱开始的地方"——在变得有用、可理解、可盈利之前。这篇帖子获得了 809 个赞，在焦虑于 agent 时代意义的社区中引起了强烈共鸣。

### Training Data — Anthropic's Katelyn Lesse & Angela Jiang: Building an Ecosystem, not a Walled Garden

**The Takeaway:** Anthropic is deliberately building its platform as an open ecosystem rather than a walled garden, with a layered architecture — knowledge → execution → coordination — that they believe will outlast any single product form factor.

**The Takeaway:** Anthropic 正在刻意将其平台构建为一个开放生态系统而非围墙花园，采用分层架构——知识 → 执行 → 协调——他们相信这将比任何单一产品形态更持久。

Katelyn Lesse (Platform Engineering) and Angela Jiang (Platform Product) from Anthropic's Platform team joined the Training Data podcast to explain how they're building "one of the most important developer platforms in the world." They describe their work as having two North Stars: internally, providing maximum leverage for Anthropic teams to ship "AGI-pilled products" quickly; externally, giving any builder the tools to work with Claude to build whatever they want.

Anthropic 平台团队的 Katelyn Lesse（平台工程）和 Angela Jiang（平台产品）加入 Training Data 播客，解释他们如何构建"世界上最重要的开发者平台之一"。他们将工作描述为有两个北极星：对内，为 Anthropic 团队提供最大杠杆，以快速交付"AGI-pilled 产品"；对外，给任何 builder 提供与 Claude 合作构建任何想要的东西的工具。

The conversation centers on a "layer cake" of abstractions: at the bottom, the knowledge layer (Messages API, skills, memory, MCP); in the middle, the execution layer (Claude Managed Agents, harnesses, infrastructure); and at the top, an emerging coordination layer where they're experimenting with "strategies" — essentially meta-harnesses that compose orchestrated agent behaviors where different tokens have different jobs (advising vs. executing, for example).

对话围绕一个"分层蛋糕"的抽象展开：底层是知识层（Messages API、skills、memory、MCP）；中间是执行层（Claude Managed Agents、harnesses、基础设施）；顶层是一个新兴的协调层，他们正在试验"strategies"——本质上是一种元 harness，编排 agent 行为，让不同的 token 承担不同的任务（例如建议 vs 执行）。

Critically, they emphasize openness over control. They've launched self-hosted sandboxes (partnering with Modal, Vercel, Cloudflare, and even Amazon's new micro VMs) and MCP tunnels so agents can call servers behind firewalls. Jiang's framing: "We actually aren't precious about you should run these things on our infrastructure... the thing that's important to us is more that the architecture of how you put together these agents in a way that will be powerful, reliable and scalable."

关键的是，他们强调开放而非控制。他们推出了自托管沙箱（与 Modal、Vercel、Cloudflare 甚至亚马逊的新微 VM 合作）和 MCP 隧道，让 agent 可以调用防火墙后的服务器。Jiang 的框架是："我们实际上并不执着于你必须在我们的基础设施上运行这些……对我们来说更重要的是你如何构建这些 agent 的架构，使其强大、可靠和可扩展。"

They also discussed the constant evolution of "form factors" — two years ago everything was chat, now it's agents, and there will be another shift. They deliberately experiment with products like Claude Design not because they expect to own every vertical, but to showcase what's possible when you let the model do more. The industries they're watching beyond coding: manufacturing ("one of our PM's getting on a flight to Detroit") and healthcare companies dealing with old systems that lack APIs.

他们还讨论了"产品形态"的不断演变——两年前一切都是 chat，现在是 agent，还会有下一次转变。他们刻意用 Claude Design 等产品做实验，不是因为他们期望拥有每个垂直领域，而是为了展示当你让模型做更多时可能实现什么。他们关注的编码之外的领域：制造业（"我们的一位 PM 正在飞往底特律"）和正在处理缺乏 API 的老系统的医疗保健公司。
