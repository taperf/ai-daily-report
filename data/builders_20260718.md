### Anthropic Engineering: How we contain Claude across products

Anthropic Engineering published a deep technical post on how they contain Claude across their three agentic products: claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork. The post argues that as agent capabilities grow, "the cost of not deploying grows large enough that the risk-reward calculation tips heavily toward adoption" — provided products can be made safe.

The article details three isolation patterns:
1. **Ephemeral containers (claude.ai)**: Code runs in gVisor containers on isolated infrastructure, server-side with no local filesystem access. Blast radius is minimal but so is the ceiling.
2. **Human-in-the-loop sandbox (Claude Code)**: Uses OS-level sandboxes (Seatbelt on macOS, bubblewrap on Linux) that reduced permission prompts by 84%. The post also reveals vulnerabilities they discovered: attackers could embed hooks in `.claude/settings.json` that executed before the trust prompt appeared, and the "user as injection vector" risk where internal red teams found ways to exploit the system.
3. **Persistent isolation (Claude Cowork)**: Designed for non-technical users with stricter boundaries and automated recovery.

Key insight: defenses must overlap across three layers — the model (classifiers, probes, training), the environment (sandboxes, VMs, egress controls), and external content (MCP servers, plugins). "The weakest layer is the one you built yourself."
🔗 https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude

Anthropic Engineering 发布了一篇深度技术文章，阐述如何在 claude.ai、Claude Code 和 Claude Cowork 三款 agentic 产品中控制 Claude 的风险范围。文章核心论点：随着 agent 能力增长，"不部署的代价变得如此之高，以至于风险收益计算严重倾向于采纳"——前提是产品能做到安全。

文章详细描述了三种隔离模式：
1. **临时容器（claude.ai）**：代码在 gVisor 容器中运行，完全服务端，无本地文件系统访问。爆炸半径极小，但能力上限也受限。
2. **人机回环沙盒（Claude Code）**：使用操作系统级沙盒（macOS 的 Seatbelt、Linux 的 bubblewrap），将权限提示减少了84%。文章还披露了发现的漏洞：攻击者可在 `.claude/settings.json` 中嵌入 hook，在用户看到信任提示前就已执行；以及内部红队发现的"用户作为注入向量"风险。
3. **持久隔离（Claude Cowork）**：面向非技术用户，边界更严格，支持自动恢复。

核心洞察：防御必须在三个层面重叠——模型层（分类器、探针、训练）、环境层（沙盒、虚拟机、出站控制）和外部内容层（MCP 服务器、插件）。"最薄弱的环节是你自己构建的那一层。"

### Unsupervised Learning — Ep 91: Top AI Analyst Unpacks Today's AI Hype Cycle

In a conversation with Redpoint's Jacob Efron, Benedict Evans — one of the most respected independent tech analysts — offered a nuanced perspective on the current AI hype cycle. Evans argued that while AI is clearly as significant as the internet and mobile, comparing it to the industrial revolution is less useful than understanding the patterns from previous tech shifts.

Key takeaways:
- **On parallels**: Evans walked through how semiconductors (Wach's law), mobile (marginal cost networks), and cloud all offer partial analogies but none have predictive power. The key lesson from mobile: networks spent $200B/year on CapEx but "all the value went up stack" — they didn't get to build Uber or YouTube. This raises the question of whether AI foundation models will similarly see value captured by application layers.
- **On consumer AI**: Evans drew a sharp distinction between daily users (software developers, knowledge workers) and the broader population. He compared the current state to "an accountant seeing the first spreadsheet in the late 70s" versus "a lawyer seeing it" — transformative for some, merely useful for others. He estimated only 10-15% of people are daily active users, with another 20-30% using it weekly or monthly.
- **On the unknown**: Unlike previous platform shifts where physical limits were understood (you knew the next iPhone wouldn't have a retinal implant), "we don't know the physical limits of this." This makes it both more exciting and harder to predict.
- **The memorable quote**: Evans dismissed overly philosophical debates about AGI by saying: "Meanwhile, let's build some software and work out what works."
🔗 https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vDY_ocrkQ5w

知名独立科技分析师 Benedict Evans 在 Redpoint 的 Jacob Efron 主持的 Unsupervised Learning 播客中，对当前 AI hype cycle 给出了细致入微的判断。Evans 认为，虽然 AI 的重要性堪比互联网和移动互联网，但将其与工业革命对比，不如理解历次技术转型的规律来得有用。

核心观点：
- **关于类比**：Evans 逐一分析了半导体（Wach's 定律）、移动网络（边际成本网络）和云计算的局部相似性，但指出这些都没有预测力。移动网络的关键教训：运营商每年投入2000亿美元资本开支，但"所有价值都向上层转移"——它们没机会做出 Uber 或 YouTube。这引发了一个问题：AI 基础模型是否也会面临应用层攫取大部分价值的命运？
- **关于消费级 AI**：Evans 在重度用户（软件开发者、知识工作者）和大众之间划出了清晰界限。他将当前状态比作"70年代末会计第一次看到电子表格" vs "律师第一次看到电子表格"——对有些人是变革性的，对另一些人只是"有点用"。他估计目前只有10-15%的人是日活用户，另有20-30%是周活或月活。
- **关于未知**：与此前平台转型不同，当时人们知道物理极限在哪里（你知道下一代 iPhone 不会有视网膜植入），但"我们不知道这项技术的物理极限在哪里"。这让前景既更令人兴奋，也更难预测。
- **金句**：对于关于 AGI 的过度哲学化争论，Evans 干脆地说："Meanwhile, let's build some software and work out what works."（"与此同时，先做点软件，看看什么管用。"）
