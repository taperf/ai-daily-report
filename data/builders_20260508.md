## 🎙️ Podcast: "Coding's Printing Press Moment" — Boris Cherny (Anthropic) on Training Data

**Show:** Training Data (Sequoia Capital)
**Guest:** Boris Cherny, creator of Claude Code
🔗 https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLOhHNjZItNnMm5tdW61JpnyxeYH5NDDx8

Boris sat down at a Sequoia event and delivered what might be the most vivid picture yet of how AI is transforming software development from the inside out. Key revelations:

**On personal workflow:** Boris now writes 100% of his code via Claude Code, mostly from his phone. His record: 150 PRs in a single day. He runs 5-10 active sessions with "a few hundred" agents during the day, and "a few thousand" doing deeper work overnight. He uses `/loop` (cron-scheduled Claude tasks) extensively — one babysits PRs and fixes CI, another keeps tests healthy, another clusters Twitter feedback every 30 minutes.

**On Claude Code's origin story:** Built as an "accidental" product within Anthropic Labs (a small incubation team that also created MCP and the desktop app). It didn't work well for six months and wasn't a hit at launch. Exponential growth started with Opus 4, then inflected with every subsequent model release (4.5, 4.6, 4.7). The team deliberately built for "the next model" — pre-PMF by design.

**On coding being "solved":** For Boris personally, it's 100% solved. For the broader world, maybe 50%. The gap isn't technology (same models available to everyone) but organizational process — Anthropic is far ahead in restructuring how teams work. Internally, "no more manually written code anywhere at the company. All SQL is written by models."

**On the "Printing Press" analogy:** Before the printing press, 10% of Europeans were literate. Within 50 years after, more literature was published than in the previous 1000 years, and book costs dropped 100x. Boris believes coding is undergoing the same democratization — but much faster than 50 years. "The best person to write accounting software is not an engineer, it's a really good accountant."

**On the SaaSpocalypse:** Some moats weaken (switching costs, process power — "Claude 4.7 can just hill-climb anything"), but others remain (network effects, scale economies, cornered resources). Prediction: 10x more startups in the next decade, because tiny teams can now compete head-to-head with incumbents.

**On the future:** Claude agents will naturally start parallelizing work, scheduling loops, and communicating with each other over Slack — without users explicitly orchestrating it. "In a year, the model will just do the right thing."

Boris谈了一场可能是迄今为止最生动的AI改变软件开发的内部视角。

**个人工作流**：Boris现在100%通过Claude Code写代码，主要在手机上操作。个人记录：一天150个PR。日常运行5-10个会话、数百个Agent，夜间运行数千个Agent做更深度的工作。他大量使用`/loop`（定时调度的Claude任务）——一个负责看管PR和修CI，一个保持测试健康，一个每30分钟聚类Twitter反馈。

**Claude Code起源**：是Anthropic Labs（同时孵化了MCP和桌面应用）的"意外产品"。前六个月效果很差，发布初期也不火。指数级增长始于Opus 4，之后每次模型升级都带来拐点。团队刻意为"下一代模型"提前构建——设计上就是pre-PMF的。

**关于编程"已解决"**：对Boris个人来说100%已解决。对更广泛的世界，大概50%。差距不在技术（大家用同样的模型），而在组织流程——Anthropic内部已经重构了团队协作方式。"公司内不再有任何手写代码，所有SQL都由模型生成。"

**印刷术类比**：印刷术发明前欧洲识字率10%；之后50年出版量超过此前1000年总和，书本成本降低100倍。编程正经历同样的民主化，但速度会快得多。"写会计软件最好的人不是工程师，是优秀的会计师。"

**SaaS末日论**：部分护城河削弱（转换成本、流程壁垒——"Claude 4.7能爬坡优化任何东西"），但另一些不变（网络效应、规模经济、稀缺资源）。预测：未来十年创业公司数量增加10倍。
