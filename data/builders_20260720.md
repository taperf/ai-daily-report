He pointed out that even with a corpus of your writing and 200 skills running in graph loops, AI produces "robotic prose… because they are robots." His sharp conclusion: "There is nothing more distasteful right now than AI replies in social media or (🟢 T H I S    T H I N G ) in landing pages. Quality and humanity will prevail."

**关于"AGI"一词：** Rauch 认为"AGI"这个词已经严重过时——AI 在"大多数经济相关任务上远比人类智能更出色"。但他强调，AI 无法替代"真正的你"。他指出，即使你提供自己的写作语料库和 200 个技能在图循环中运行，AI 产出的仍然是"机器人般的散文……因为它们就是机器人"。他的尖锐结论："现在没有什么比社交媒体上的 AI 回复或着陆页上的那种🟢 T H I S    T H I N G 更令人反感了。质量和人性终将胜出。"

### Box CEO Aaron Levie: AI ecosystem diffusion + China competition

**On ecosystem value:** Levie wrote a long-form analysis arguing that the past few months represent a turning point in how AI value will be distributed. "If you thought the value of the AI ecosystem was going to only accrue to a few companies, the past few months have represented a turning point." He outlined five emerging categories: (1) enterprise model-tuning and inference providers, (2) applied AI companies delivering end-user experience across legal/IT/security/HR/coding, (3) new domain-specific labs in life sciences/finance/healthcare, (4) infrastructure for running models and agents, and (5) services firms driving enterprise change management. "Way too early to call the winning architectures, and in reality it's going to be a heterogenous environment."

**关于生态系统价值：** Levie 发表了一篇长篇分析，认为过去几个月标志着 AI 价值分配方式的转折点。"如果你认为 AI 生态系统的价值只会积累到少数公司手中，那么过去几个月已经代表了一个转折点。"他梳理了五个新兴类别：(1) 企业模型微调和推理服务提供商，(2) 在法律/IT/安全/人力资源/编程等领域交付终端用户体验的应用 AI 公司，(3) 生命科学/金融/医疗等领域的新垂直实验室，(4) 运行模型和 Agent 的基础设施，以及 (5) 推动企业变革管理的服务公司。"现在判断胜出的架构还为时过早，实际上这将是一个异构环境。"

### Zara Zhang: "Personal eval set" + enterprise AI adoption barrier

**On personal evals:** Zhang advised everyone to develop their own "personal eval set" for AI models — "a few tasks that are actually relevant to your day-to-day work/life." She noted that industry benchmarks might not reflect what will actually make a model useful to you personally. "You find the model's capability boundary by poking at it & bumping into it for fun."

**关于个人评估：** Zhang 建议每个人都应该为自己的 AI 模型建立一个"个人评估集"——"几个与你日常工作/生活真正相关的任务"。她指出，行业基准测试可能无法反映什么会让模型对你个人真正有用。"通过戳它、碰它来找到模型的能力边界，这本身就是一种乐趣。"

### Unsupervised Learning: AI Pioneer Jürgen Schmidhuber on the State of AI Today

**The Takeaway:** The godfather of modern neural networks believes today's AI CapEx boom is massively overdone and a stock market correction is coming, yet remains deeply optimistic about AI technology itself — while arguing that true AGI requires physical robots, not just screen-based intelligence.

**核心观点：** 现代神经网络之父认为，当今 AI 资本支出泡沫严重过度，股市调整即将到来，但他对 AI 技术本身仍然深感乐观——同时主张真正的 AGI 需要物理机器人，而不仅仅是屏幕上的智能。

Jürgen Schmidhuber — the researcher behind LSTM, meta-learning, and other foundational advances that power today's AI revolution — sat down with Jacob Efron for a wide-ranging conversation. Schmidhuber's core argument: the AI behind the screen is working well, but "true AI is also real robots, real machinery in the physical world, and that's not working as well." He compared human hands — "full of sensors, millions of little sensors... you cut it, and it starts healing itself" — to current robot hardware, concluding that "you can't have AGI without hardware like that."

Jürgen Schmidhuber——LSTM、元学习及其他驱动当今 AI 革命的基础性突破的幕后研究者——与 Jacob Efron 进行了一场广泛而深入的对话。Schmidhuber 的核心论点是：屏幕背后的 AI 运行良好，但"真正的 AI 也是现实世界的机器人、真实机械，而在这方面做得还不够好"。他将人类的手——"充满传感器，数百万个小传感器……你割伤了它，它开始自我修复"——与当前的机器人硬件对比，得出结论："没有这样的硬件，你不可能拥有 AGI。"

On recursive self-improvement (RSI), Schmidhuber traced his work from 1987 meta-evolution through the 2003 "Gödel machine" (a mathematically optimal self-improver) to today's neural network weight-modification approaches. He believes current RSI methods are "scaled back versions" of the Gödel machine, limited by gradient descent. He also rejected the idea that RSI will create a moat for big companies: "many of the guys who are interested in self improvement, they are open source guys. Everybody's cooking with the same water."

关于递归自我改进（RSI），Schmidhuber 梳理了他从 1987 年的元进化到 2003 年的"哥德尔机"（一种数学上最优的自我改进器），再到今天的神经网络权重修改方法的研究脉络。他认为当前的 RSI 方法是哥德尔机的"缩减版本"，受限于梯度下降。他还否定了 RSI 会为大型公司创造护城河的观点："许多对自我改进感兴趣的人都是开源阵营的。每个人都在用同一锅水做饭。"

Perhaps his most provocative take: the current trillion-dollar data center buildout is unsustainable. "The guys who are investing a thousand billion dollars into GPUs for data centers today, within the next five years, they are going to lose $900 billion." He argued that compute per dollar improves roughly 10x every five years, making today's massive investments obsolete quickly. "There will be one of these stock market crashes because at the moment, I think there's a lot of misallocation."

也许他最具挑衅性的观点：当前万亿美元的数据中心建设是不可持续的。"今天在数据中心 GPU 上投资一万亿美元的人，在未来五年内，他们将损失九千亿美元。"他认为，每美元计算能力大约每五年提升 10 倍，使今天的大规模投资迅速过时。"将会有一次股市崩盘，因为目前我认为存在大量资本错配。"

On AI safety, Schmidhuber was notably less worried than many in the field. He called 2010s alignment efforts "naive" because they assumed a single objective function could align AI with "the needs of humans" — but "if you have 10 different humans in one room, they all will have different needs." He argued that since 1990, his lab has built systems that "invent their own new objective functions" and are therefore less predictable, but "not going to be more dangerous than what you have now with humans." His ultimate reassurance: artificial scientists will be "fascinated by life and they will be greatly motivated to protect the source of interesting patterns rather than destroy it."

关于 AI 安全，Schmidhuber 比该领域许多人士明显不那么担忧。他称 2010 年代的对齐努力"天真"，因为它们假设一个单一的目标函数就能使 AI 与"人类的需求"对齐——但"如果你在一个房间里有 10 个不同的人，他们都会有不同的需求。"他认为，自 1990 年以来，他的实验室已经建立了"发明自己新目标函数"的系统，因此可预测性较低，但"不会比现在的人类更危险。"他最终的 reassurance：人工科学家将"对生命着迷，并有巨大的动力去保护有趣模式的源头，而不是摧毁它。"
