**No Priors: How Nuclear Will Unlock Energy Abundance with Valar Atomics Founder Isaiah Taylor**

The Takeaway: AI's insatiable appetite for compute is creating a once-in-a-generation tailwind for nuclear energy startups — and the founder who can deliver cheap, abundant power may capture the most strategically important market of the century.

Isaiah Taylor, founder of Valar Atomics, has just pulled off something remarkable: his startup built the first advanced reactor to generate power in the US in over fifty years, and it's already powering AI chips. The conversation with No Priors host Sarah Guo cuts through the usual energy policy noise to focus on what matters: hardware iteration speed, cost, and safety.

Taylor's core argument is that most of the nuclear industry is a "modeling and simulation industry" — obsessed with theoretical safety, not hardware iteration. Valar's approach is the opposite: build the simplest, safest reactor possible, then iterate fast. The safety data is striking: nuclear is already empirically the safest form of energy generation, even safer than solar when you account for installation deaths. But Taylor wants to go further by decoupling risk probability from consequence severity — designing systems where even worst-case scenarios have bounded outcomes.

The energy-compute nexus is the real story here. When SpaceX started, people said the demand wasn't there. Taylor argues energy is the same: it's a commodity where price drives demand. If you can make energy at 1¢, you induce your own demand. A tenth of a cent, even more. The implication is that AI compute's power hunger isn't just a constraint — it's a demand engine that could fund an entire new energy infrastructure. Valar is building "gigasites" — massive power campuses — on the theory that if you provide cheap power at scale, the load will come to you. For anyone tracking the AI infrastructure stack, this is a must-listen.
