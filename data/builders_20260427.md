## PODCASTS

**No Priors: Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott**

The Takeaway: Enterprise AI adoption is finally moving past the experiment phase, but the real winner won't be the model — it'll be whoever owns the workflow.

ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott makes the case that workflow platforms are the essential complement to LLMs, not their victim. His sharpest argument: replacing a simple ServiceNow application with a language model costs 10x more when you factor in GPU costs, token pricing, human capital, and the rebuilding effort. "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake."

McDermott reveals that 90% of ServiceNow's customer service cases are now handled by agents, major customers are going live in under 30 days (down from months), and he expects 2.2 billion AI agents to enter the workforce in the next couple years. On headcount: "The net new added headcount will be dramatically reduced... the agents are going to be able to do a lot of that." His advice to employees everywhere: "Make sure what you're capable of doing can't be easily replicated by an agent."

Perhaps most revealing: only 11% of companies globally have moved past AI experiments into production. The first movers are aggressive and focused on one thing — changing their business model, not just adding AI features.

ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott认为，工作流平台是LLM的必要补充，而非受害者。他最锋利的论点：用语言模型替代一个简单的ServiceNow应用，算上GPU成本、Token费用、人力资本和重建工程，成本是10倍。"经营企业的人理解人会犯错，但他们永远不会原谅软件犯错。"

McDermott透露，ServiceNow 90%的客服案例现已由Agent处理，大客户30天内即可上线（以前要数月），他预计未来几年将有22亿AI Agent进入劳动力市场。关于人员编制："新增人数将大幅减少……Agent将承担大量工作。"给所有员工的建议："确保你能做的事不会被Agent轻易替代。"

最值得关注的数据：全球只有11%的企业已将AI从实验阶段推进到生产环境。先行者非常激进，且聚焦于一件事——改变商业模式，而不仅仅是给现有流程加个AI功能。
