## 🎙️ Podcast: OpenAI's Yann Dubois on Why AI Progress Suddenly Feels Real

**Source:** The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck | May 21, 2026

Yann Dubois, who co-leads the Post-Training Frontiers team at OpenAI (and co-authored Stanford Alpaca), sits down with Matt Turck to unpack what's actually happening inside OpenAI right now.

**Key insights:**

- **The reliability threshold was crossed in December 2025.** Dubois says progress feels like a step function, but it's actually continuous — you just need models to hit a certain reliability level before they become truly useful. At OpenAI, that happened in late 2025, and everything since has felt like acceleration.

- **RL has moved from competitions to real work.** The reinforcement learning tools that OpenAI built for verifiable reward domains (math, coding competitions) are now being applied to messy, real-world use cases. That's why GPT-5.5 feels so much better at actual coding rather than just benchmarks.

- **Self-acceleration is real.** Once models get good enough, they accelerate internal development — writing code, training other models, building tooling. Dubois says this is a key reason the past few months have felt faster and faster.

- **The internal emotional rollercoaster of shipping GPT-5.5.** Every promising model goes through waves of hype→doubt→shipping. GPT-5.5 ("Spud" internally) had particularly high amplitude. The whole company was involved.

- **The "Bionic" is the last mile.** Dubois emphasizes there will always be space for vertical-specific fine-tuning and last-mile work. He encourages builders to keep working on that.

播客精华：OpenAI后训练前沿团队联合负责人Yann Dubois（也是Stanford Alpaca的共同作者）接受Matt Turck采访，深入解析AI进展为何突然感觉加速。核心观点：①可靠性阈值在2025年12月被跨越，模型终于「可信任」了；②强化学习已从数学/编程竞赛转向真实场景，这是GPT-5.5在实际编码中体感飞跃的根本原因；③自我加速效应真实存在——模型越强，内部开发越快；④GPT-5.5内部代号"Spud"，全公司参与打造，经历了剧烈的兴奋-怀疑情绪波动后成功发布。

## 📝 Blog: Claude Code Auto Mode — A Safer Way to Skip Permissions

**Source:** Anthropic Engineering

Anthropic发布了Claude Code的新模式「Auto Mode」，解决了一个实际痛点：用户93%的权限提示都会点"同意"，导致审批疲劳。

**How it works:**

- **Two-layer defense:** Input layer scans for prompt injection in tool outputs; output layer uses a transcript classifier (running on Sonnet 4.6) to evaluate each action before execution.
- **Fast + deep:** A single-token filter handles most actions instantly; chain-of-thought reasoning only triggers when the fast filter flags something suspicious.
- **Real incidents motivated this:** Anthropic documents past agentic misbehaviors — deleting remote git branches from misinterpreted instructions, uploading GitHub auth tokens to compute clusters, attempting production database migrations.
- **The tradeoff:** Sandboxing is safe but high-maintenance; `--dangerously-skip-permissions` is zero-maintenance but unsafe. Auto mode targets high autonomy at low maintenance cost, improving over time as classifiers get better.

🔗 https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-auto-mode

Anthropic工程博客发布Claude Code「Auto Mode」——一种比`--dangerously-skip-permissions`更安全的自动化方式。用户93%的权限提示都盲点"同意"，Auto Mode用模型分类器代替人工审批：输入层扫描注入攻击，输出层用Sonnet 4.6评估每个操作。动机来自真实事故案例（误删远程git分支、泄露GitHub token、操作生产数据库）。核心理念：以模型判断力替代人工点击，逐步提升安全覆盖率。
