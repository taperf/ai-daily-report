## 🎙️ PODCASTS

### The MAD Podcast: "Why AWS and Azure Cannot Run Autonomous AI" — Ivan Burazin (Daytona)

**The Takeaway:** Every AI agent needs its own computer (sandbox), and the hyperscalers' existing infrastructure isn't designed for this — creating a massive opportunity for specialized infra.

Ivan Burazin, CEO of Daytona, breaks down why AI agents need dedicated sandboxes from first principles. His core argument: agents are "digital knowledge workers" — they need their own machines, accounts, even phone numbers (for 2FA) to function safely and effectively. Giving an agent access to your personal computer is a security nightmare (his agent tried to access his bank).

Key insights from the episode:
- **Architecture:** Daytona threw out Kubernetes and wrote their own scheduler because K8s wasn't designed for ephemeral, agent-driven workloads
- **Scale thesis:** Every agent will need at least one sandbox, sometimes more. This implies a potential global CPU shortage
- **Security model:** Sandboxes provide isolation — the worst case is data leakage, not unauthorized spending (agents get limited accounts)
- **OpenClaw connection:** The OpenClaw + Mac Mini setup helped people finally understand what "sandbox for agents" means in practice
- **Go-to-market lessons:** 16 years of developer tool startups taught Ivan that awareness → understanding is the hardest gap to bridge

**关键要点**：每个AI Agent都需要自己的计算机（沙箱），而超大规模云厂商现有基础设施并非为此设计——这创造了巨大的专业基础设施机会。

Daytona CEO Ivan Burazin从第一性原理解释了为什么AI Agent需要专属沙箱。核心论点：Agent是"数字知识工作者"——它们需要自己的机器、账号甚至电话号码（用于2FA）才能安全有效地工作。让Agent访问你的个人电脑是安全噩梦（他的Agent试图访问银行账户）。

核心洞察：
- **架构选择**：Daytona放弃了Kubernetes，自研调度器，因为K8s不是为短暂的Agent驱动工作负载设计的
- **规模论断**：每个Agent至少需要一个沙箱，有时需要更多——这暗示全球CPU短缺可能比想象中来得更快
- **安全模型**：沙箱提供隔离——最坏情况是数据泄露而非未授权支出（Agent获得受限账号）
- **分发经验**：16年开发者工具创业教会他，从"知道"到"理解"是最难跨越的鸿沟
