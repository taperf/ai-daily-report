**Andrej Karpathy** (former Director of AI at Tesla, OpenAI founding team)

Karpathy shared a compelling vision for the future of human-AI interaction, arguing that the current text/markdown output paradigm is just the beginning. His suggestion: ask your LLM to "structure your response as HTML" and view it in a browser — it works surprisingly well. He outlined a progression from raw text → markdown → HTML → ultimately interactive neural videos/simulations generated by diffusion models. On the input side, he notes we still lack the ability to point/gesture at things on screen, similar to how we'd interact with a person physically next to us. "Around a third of our brains are a massively parallel processor dedicated to vision — it is the 10-lane superhighway of information into brain."

Guest: Alexander Taubman, Co-founder & CEO of Long Lake Management

The Takeaway: Long Lake just announced a $6.3B acquisition of American Express Global Business Travel — potentially the world's first "AI take-private" — built on a thesis that buying and transforming legacy service businesses with AI creates dramatically better outcomes than selling them software.

Long Lake 的模式颠覆了传统PE和SaaS逻辑：不卖软件、不裁员降本，而是买下公司后用自研AI平台 Nexus 让员工获得"超能力"，从而驱动增长。核心insight包括：

- **AI不是削减成本，而是增长引擎**：收购的公司从年增0-5%提升到20%+，因为AI让现有团队能服务更多客户，增量收入的边际成本极低

Taubman的金句："AI is incredibly positive sum. We actually think AI makes people more productive. And when you have more productive people, you want more of them."
