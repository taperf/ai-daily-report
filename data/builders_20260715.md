### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie laid out his structural map of where AI is heading. Frontier labs will keep pushing the intelligence ceiling, while open-weight models rapidly absorb their breakthroughs — creating a "healthy counterbalance" that lets enterprises run models at cost and fine-tune for specific workflows. The real opportunity, he argues, is in the Applied AI layer: combining frontier intelligence with cheaper models through smart model routing, which he calls "the template for the future." On enterprise models specifically, Levie pushes back against the idea that every company will train its own — the most valuable enterprise data is also the most sensitive, and you can't pack your security layer inside the model itself.

Box CEO Aaron Levie 梳理了 AI 行业未来结构趋势。前沿实验室将持续推高智能上限，而开源权重模型会快速吸收这些突破，形成健康的制衡——让企业能以成本价运行模型并针对工作流微调。他认为真正的机会在应用 AI 层：通过智能模型路由将前沿智能与低成本模型结合，这是未来的标准模板。关于企业自有模型，Levie 反对"每家公司都会训练自己的模型"的观点——企业最有价值的数据往往也最敏感，而安全层无法内嵌到模型本身里。

### Claude Blog: Building Intelligent Apps for Apple Platforms

Anthropic released a new Swift package that lets Apple developers use Apple's Foundation Models framework to call Claude for more complex workflows. The integration allows handoffs from on-device Apple Intelligence (for fast local tasks like summarization) to Claude (for multi-step reasoning, code generation, and web search). Because Apple's framework returns typed Swift values via @Generable annotations, developers arrive at the Claude API call with clean structured inputs instead of raw user text. This is a significant developer experience improvement — bridging Apple's on-device AI with cloud-based frontier intelligence through a unified, type-safe interface. Available on iOS 27, iPadOS 27, macOS 27, visionOS 27, and watchOS 27.

Anthropic 发布了新的 Swift 包，让 Apple 开发者可以通过 Apple 的 Foundation Models 框架调用 Claude 处理更复杂的任务。该集成允许从设备端的 Apple Intelligence（用于快速本地任务如摘要）无缝切换至 Claude（用于多步推理、代码生成和网页搜索）。由于 Apple 框架通过 @Generable 注解返回类型化的 Swift 值，开发者调用 Claude API 时获得的是结构化输入而非原始用户文本。这是开发者体验的重大改进——通过一个统一的类型安全接口，将 Apple 设备端 AI 与云端前沿智能桥接起来。支持 iOS 27、iPadOS 27、macOS 27、visionOS 27 和 watchOS 27。

### The MAD Podcast: Inside Nemotron & NVIDIA's AI Lab | Bryan Catanzaro

**The Takeaway:** NVIDIA's open-source model effort is not a side project — it's a core strategic bet on understanding AI from first principles to build better chips and software, and on the long-term safety of open technologies over walled gardens.

Bryan Catanzaro, who leads NVIDIA's Nemotron family of open foundation models, sat down with Matt Turck for a deep dive into where open-source AI stands, how Nemotron models are built, and why NVIDIA is in the model business at all. Catanzaro made a compelling case: Nemotron has two jobs. First, to help NVIDIA understand AI deeply enough to codesign its entire stack — from transistors to algorithms. "Moore's Law is dead," he said flatly, "has been for years." In this era, the only way to get more intelligence is to be more efficient, and that requires understanding the workloads from first principles. Second, Nemotron exists to support the open ecosystem — not because NVIDIA wants to be the only player, but because "whenever AI is further developed and further deployed, it's an opportunity for our business."

On Nemotron 3 Ultra — which briefly became the top US open-weight model — Catanzaro walked through the technical stack: hybrid Mamba-transformer architecture, mixture of experts, multi-token prediction, multi-teacher distillation, and 4-bit training. He also revealed the messy marketing reality of overlapping version numbers (Nemotron 4 is coming, but there was already a Nemotron 4 in 2024). Most striking was his closing argument on safety: open technologies are safer because "there's more sunlight." He framed monoculture as the real danger — "we tried that" — and argued that diversity of thought, even around deeply felt disagreements, is inherently safer than a walled garden where a small group decides what's safe for everyone else.

**Key quote:** "I believe that open technologies for AI are inherently the safest way of building AI."

Bryan Catanzaro 领导 NVIDIA 的 Nemotron 开源基础模型家族，他在与 Matt Turck 的深度对话中阐述了开源 AI 的现状、Nemotron 的构建方式，以及 NVIDIA 为何要做模型。Catanzaro 提出了一个有力的论点：Nemotron 有两个使命。第一，帮助 NVIDIA 深入理解 AI，以便从晶体管到算法协同设计整个技术栈。"摩尔定律已经死了，"他平静地说，"已经死了好几年了。"在这个时代，获得更多智能的唯一方式是提高效率，而这需要从第一性原理理解工作负载。第二，Nemotron 的存在是为了支持开放生态系统——不是因为 NVIDIA 想成为唯一玩家，而是因为"AI 越发展、越部署，对我们的业务就是机会。"

关于 Nemotron 3 Ultra——它一度成为美国顶尖开源权重模型——Catanzaro 详细解读了技术栈：混合 Mamba-Transformer 架构、混合专家模型（MoE）、多 token 预测、多教师蒸馏，以及 4-bit 训练。他还透露了版本号重叠的营销混乱现实（Nemotron 4 即将到来，但 2024 年已经有一个 Nemotron 4）。最引人注目的是他关于安全性的总结陈词：开放技术更安全，因为"阳光更多"。他将单一文化视为真正的危险——"我们试过那样"——并认为思想多样性，即便在存在深刻分歧的领域，也比由一小群人决定什么对所有人安全的"围墙花园"更安全。

**关键引用：**"我相信开放技术从根本上是构建 AI 最安全的方式。"
