**Box CEO Aaron Levie**

Aaron delivered three substantive takes worth noting. First, on AI regulation: in a world where strong open source alternatives are only just behind frontier models, gatekeeping access to frontier capabilities makes you less secure, not more. Even if the US could fully ban open source models, other ecosystems wouldn't — and they'd gain a competitive edge. The calculus of AI regulation must change to account for open weights models being far stronger than expected.

Second, on AI economics: when AI costs drop, spend on AI doesn't decrease — it increases. Cheaper AI gets consumed more, across a wider range of tasks. You write more code, review more for bugs and security, run agents on larger datasets. For the foreseeable future, anything that lowers token costs will drive up inference demand. This also explains why open source business models work in AI — nobody is running these models on devices; they're running them in infra.

Third, on AI diffusion: progress driven by AI will be rate-limited by its interaction with the real world. Coding adoption is fast because code can be written, tested, and run end-to-end by a single person without external dependencies. Life sciences, sales, contracts — all require real-world feedback loops that don't care how smart the model is. "The only way to find out is to build the blade and try to break it." This is why there's enormous opportunity in the applied AI layer — model outputs alone aren't enough; you need to change underlying workflows and build systems that handle real-world feedback.

Box CEO Aaron Levie 提出了三个值得关注的观点。第一，关于 AI 监管：在开源模型仅略落后于前沿模型的世界里，限制前沿能力的使用反而降低自身安全性。即使美国能完全禁用开源模型，其他生态不会跟进，反而获得竞争优势。AI 监管的思路必须改变。

第二，关于 AI 经济学：AI 成本下降时，AI 支出不降反增。更便宜的 AI 会被用在更多场景——写更多代码、做更多安全审查、处理更大规模数据。可预见的未来里，任何降低 token 成本的举措都会推高推理需求。这也解释了为什么开源商业模式在 AI 领域可行——没人在本地跑模型，都在云端跑。

第三，关于 AI 扩散：AI 驱动的进步最终受限于其与现实世界的交互。编程之所以被快速采用，是因为一个人就能端到端地写、测、运行代码。但生命科学、销售、合同谈判都需要现实世界反馈——"唯一的验证方式是造出涡轮叶片然后试着折断它"。这就是应用 AI 层的巨大机会所在——光有模型输出不够，需要改变底层工作流并建立处理现实反馈的系统。

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck: Stripe's AI Chief: How AI Agents Will Buy, Sell, and Pay**

*Guest: Emily Sense, Head of Data and AI at Stripe*

**The Takeaway:** Agentic commerce is no longer hypothetical — Stripe has real infrastructure deployed with Google, OpenAI, Meta, and major brands, but the truly transformative shift will come when agents run entire businesses on their own, buying and selling at machine speed with streaming micropayments.

Emily Sense, Stripe's Head of Data and AI, returns to the MAD Podcast to map how agentic commerce has evolved from theory to deployed infrastructure over the past year. Stripe has built an "agentic commerce suite" with three core components: the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), co-created with OpenAI, which lets businesses expose product catalogs once and opt into all compatible agents; the Shared Payment Token, a new payment primitive that lets consumers authorize agents to pay on their behalf without exposing actual card details; and the Link Wallet for agents, Stripe's consumer wallet (300M users) adapted so agents can make payments with programmable guardrails — spending limits, merchant restrictions, geographic scope, per-transaction approval toggles.

The most under-discussed topic in AI, according to Emily, is token theft. Fraudsters have realized they don't need to steal money or credentials — they can steal tokens, which have real value for building, reselling, or wrapping into new products. More than one in six signups at AI companies are multi-account abuse. Free trial abuse has more than doubled on Stripe in six months. There are cottage industries selling "free trial cards" that expire in 24 hours. Fraudsters mass-generate music tracks using stolen tokens, upload them to Spotify, and collect royalties with fake streams. Some literally clone AI products using vibe-coded websites that resell stolen tokens at a discount. Stripe's Radar fraud prevention product has evolved to combat this, leveraging the density of the Stripe network — since basically all AI buyers and sellers are on Stripe, they've seen most good and bad actors before.

On monetization, Emily argues that per-seat SaaS pricing is fundamentally broken for AI companies because every API call has real marginal cost. The pattern she sees: companies start with simple subscriptions (Lovable, ElevenLabs), then evolve to hybrid billing — a fixed fee up to a threshold, then usage-based billing tied to actual token consumption. With agents consuming at machine speed, real-time metering and real-time billing become essential, which is why Stripe partnered with Metronome (real-time usage tracking) and Tempo (blockchain optimized for streaming micropayments in stablecoins).

Perhaps the most striking macro insight: new business registrations are surging globally — up 40% in Netherlands, 70% in Finland, 80% in France. The pace of new businesses launching on Stripe has doubled since last year. Atlas (Stripe's incorporation product) startups from the 2026 cohort are tracking to 5x the revenue of last year's class at the same age. The growth is driven by vibe coding making building accessible, vibe deployment (Stripe's new Projects product orchestrates signup and integration across Vercel, Supabase, Cloudflare, Twilio etc. from the command line), and AI-powered operations enabling solopreneurs. There are now 5 million Americans making their living running solo companies, with hundreds of thousands clearing $1M/year. Emily sees AI not concentrating the market among a few giants, but enabling an explosion of small, fast-growing, globally-distributed businesses.

> "Fraudsters have figured out that in AI, you actually don't really need to steal money or credentials. You can just steal tokens. And the scale of this actually shocked me when I looked at the data. So more than one in six signups at AI companies are this kind of abuse."

> "When you make AI cheaper, it will get consumed more. Because now you can afford to use it for a wider range of tasks than you did before. Thus, for the foreseeable future, anything that lowers the cost of tokens will drive up inference demand."

> "The incremental growth [in new businesses] is coming entirely from non-employer firms — you and I would just call them solopreneurs. The number of solopreneurs earning more than $100,000 a year has gone like this since 2022. There are 5 million people in America making their living running solo companies."

MAD Podcast 主持人 Matt Turck 与 Stripe 数据与 AI 负责人 Emily Sense 深度对话，描绘了 Agent 商务从理论到落地基础设施的一年。

**核心要点：** Agent 商务已不再是假设——Stripe 与 Google、OpenAI、Meta 及主要品牌部署了真实的基础设施，但真正的变革性飞跃将出现在 Agent 能够自主运营整个业务、以机器速度进行买卖和流式微支付的时代。

Stripe 构建了"Agent 商务套件"，包含三个核心组件：与 OpenAI 联合创建的 Agent 商务协议（ACP），让企业一次性暴露产品目录即可接入所有兼容 Agent；共享支付令牌（Shared Payment Token），让消费者授权 Agent 代付而无需暴露真实卡片信息；以及 Link 钱包的 Agent 版本——Stripe 3 亿用户的消费钱包增加了可编程护栏（消费限额、商户限制、地理范围、逐笔审批开关）。

Emily 认为 AI 领域最被低估的话题是 token 盗窃。欺诈者发现不需要偷钱或凭证——偷 token 就行，因为 token 有真实价值，可以用来构建、转售或包装成新产品。AI 公司超过六分之一的新注册是恶意多账号滥用。免费试用欺诈半年内翻了一倍多。有人批量生成音乐曲目上传到 Spotify，再用假播放量赚取版税。还有人用 vibe code 克隆 AI 产品网站，以折扣价转售偷来的 token。Stripe 的 Radar 欺诈防护产品已进化到全客户生命周期覆盖，利用 Stripe 网络的密度——因为几乎所有 AI 买家和卖家都在 Stripe 上，好人和坏人都见过。

在定价模式上，Emily 指出按席位收费的 SaaS 模式对 AI 公司根本行不通，因为每次 API 调用都有真实边际成本。典型路径：公司从简单订阅开始（如 Lovable、ElevenLabs），然后演变为混合计费——固定费用到一定门槛后按实际 token 消耗计费。随着 Agent 以机器速度消费，实时计量和实时计费变得必不可少，这也是 Stripe 与 Metronome（实时用量跟踪）和 Tempo（用于流式微支付的区块链）合作的原因。

最引人注目的宏观洞察：全球新企业注册量激增——荷兰增长 40%，芬兰 70%，法国 80%。Stripe 上新企业上线速度较去年翻倍。Atlas（Stripe 的公司注册产品）2026 届初创企业的收入轨迹是去年同期的 5 倍。增长动力来自 vibe coding 降低了构建门槛、vibe deployment（Stripe 新推出的 Projects 产品从命令行编排 Vercel、Supabase、Cloudflare、Twilio 等服务的注册和集成），以及 AI 驱动的运营赋能超级个体。美国现有 500 万人靠运营一人公司为生，其中数十万人年收入超过 100 万美元。

> "欺诈者发现在 AI 领域，你其实不需要偷钱或凭证，偷 token 就行了。我看到数据时真的很震惊——AI 公司超过六分之一的注册是这种滥用。"

> "当你让 AI 更便宜，它的消费量会上升而不是下降。因为现在你能负担更多场景的使用。可预见的未来里，任何降低 token 成本的举措都会推高推理需求。"
